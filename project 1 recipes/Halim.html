Halim Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup wheat
  • 1/2 cup lentils
  • 200g meat (optional)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups water or broth
  • Fresh cilantro and fried onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the wheat and lentils thoroughly. Soak them in water for at least 2 hours or overnight for best results.
  2. If using meat, cut it into small pieces. In a large pot, heat some oil and sauté the meat until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add more oil if needed and sauté the chopped onions until golden brown. Add garlic and ginger paste, cooking for another minute.
  4. Add turmeric, cumin, coriander powder, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine the spices with the onion mixture.
  5. Drain the soaked wheat and lentils, then add them to the pot. Stir to coat them with the spices.
  6. Add water or broth to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer, covered, for about 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Add more water if necessary.
  7. If using meat, add it back into the pot during the last hour of cooking.
  8. Once the wheat and lentils are soft and have a porridge-like consistency, adjust seasoning as needed.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and fried onions.

Tips
  • Soaking the wheat and lentils beforehand helps them cook faster and more evenly.
  • Stirring occasionally while simmering prevents the halim from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  • Adjust the consistency by adding more water or broth if needed.
  • For a vegetarian version, you can skip the meat and add more lentils and vegetables.
  • Halim tastes even better the next day, so consider making it in advance.

Enjoy your delicious Halim!
